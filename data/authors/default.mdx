Zhiheng Wang is an AI Engineer and Master of Computer Science candidate at Rice University. With a rigorous foundation in competitive programming (ICPC Silver, NOIP 1st Prize), his current focus lies in bridging the gap between cutting-edge AI research and production-grade engineering applications.

He recently served as a Research Intern at the Beijing Academy of Artificial Intelligence (BAAI). There, he architected scalable data pipelines using vLLM and FlagScale, deployed MCP services, and fine-tuned models like Qwen3 to accelerate machine learning experimentation.

His core engineering interests include Model Orchestration, Agentic Workflows, and Multimodal Machine Learning. He actively develops privacy-first AI agents—such as long-context memory engines utilizing Mem0 and ChromaDB—and is currently exploring the construction of Multimodal Knowledge Graphs (MMKG) using Vision-Language Models and PyTorch Geometric.

Beyond orchestrating LLMs and building backend AI infrastructures, Zhiheng enjoys developing interactive full-stack web demos and writing about his engineering iterations.
